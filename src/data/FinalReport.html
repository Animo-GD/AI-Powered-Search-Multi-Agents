Procurement Report – NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1050 Ti 4GB

Executive Summary

This report summarizes the procurement process for the NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1050 Ti 4GB graphics cards sourced from Sigma Computer. Five models were identified, with prices ranging from 4,250 EGP to 6,000 EGP. Three models are currently out of stock, with discounts applied only to the first item. Recommendations focus on securing the highest‑value item at the lowest price while monitoring availability.

Introduction

The purpose of this report is to evaluate the available options for the GTX 1050 Ti 4GB graphics card, identify the best purchase decision, and propose procurement actions for the IT procurement team of the company.

Methodology

  1. Conducted a targeted Google Site search on sigma-computer.com for the keyword GTX 1050 Ti 4GB.
  2. Extracted product listings from the search results and captured relevant fields: title, URL, price, discount, and availability notes.
  3. Assigned a relevance rank based on search score and product match.
  4. Compiled data into a structured JSON of product objects.
  5. Generated visualization charts using Chart.js for quick price and availability comparison.

Analysis

The first item—Manli GeForce GTX 1050 Ti NVIDIA 4GB GDDR5—offers a 14 % discount reducing the price from the undisclosed original price to 4,250 EGP. It is the only item listed as “In stock”, making it the most reliable choice for immediate deployment. The other four items, while priced modestly (4,299 – 4,499 EGP) or comparatively higher (6,000 EGP), are marked as out of stock. This limits options for meeting tight project timelines. No product shows a higher discount than the top rank product. Consequently, the price–value balance favors the first product for short‑term procurement. For a longer‑term strategy, we recommend following the stock status of the other listings and re‑evaluating when they come back in inventory, as they offer slightly better performance (OC / D5 variants) at a comparable cost.

Recommendations

  • Proceed with the purchase of the Manli GeForce GTX 1050 Ti NVIDIA 4GB as the priority item (Rank 1). It offers the highest discount and is in stock.
  • Set up a reorder trigger on Sigma Computer for the out-of-stock items (Rk 2–5) to capture potential supply refreshes.
  • Negotiate a supplier contract with Sigma Computer for bulk discounts and extended warranty.
  • Evaluate alternative suppliers (e.g., local retail chains, Amazon Egypt) to broaden the price/availability comparison.
  • Document the procurement decision and the rationale to the Governance Board for audit purposes.

Conclusion

The procurement team can confidently acquire the top‑ranked GTX 1050 Ti product within the company’s budget while ensuring on‑time delivery. Ongoing monitoring of the secondary suppliers will enhance resilience against stock shortages.
